The global financial crisis of 2007-2009 unfolded in a sequence of escalating shocks that transformed a problem in one segment of the American mortgage market into the most severe global financial crisis since the Great Depression. The crisis was not a single event but a process that extended over approximately eighteen months, from the first signs of stress in the subprime market in early 2007 through the panic of September-October 2008 and the deep recession that followed.

This session provides a detailed chronological account of the crisis as it unfolded. It traces the sequence from the initial cracks in the subprime market, through the freeze in interbank lending markets, the rescues and failures of major financial institutions, the global contagion, and the transmission of the financial crisis to the real economy. The theoretical frameworks of Sessions 2 and 3, the Diamond-Dybvig run, the financial accelerator, the fire-sale mechanism, the sudden stop, and the sovereign-bank nexus, will be seen operating simultaneously and reinforcing each other in real time.

The policy response to the crisis is covered in Session 11. This session focuses on the crisis itself: what happened, why, and in what sequence.

## Learning Objectives

After completing this session, you should be able to:

1. Describe the sequence of events from the first subprime losses in early 2007 through the freeze in the ABCP and interbank lending markets in August 2007.
2. Explain why Bear Stearns failed and why the authorities chose to arrange its rescue rather than allow it to collapse.
3. Provide a detailed account of the September 2008 panic, including the conservatorship of Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, the bankruptcy of Lehman Brothers, and the rescue of AIG, and explain the consequences of each decision.
4. Analyze the run on the shadow banking system using the Diamond-Dybvig framework, explaining the roles of repo markets, money market funds, and commercial paper.
5. Explain how the crisis spread from the United States to the rest of the world through trade, financial, and confidence channels.
6. Describe the mechanisms through which the financial crisis was transmitted to the real economy, producing the deepest recession since the 1930s.

## Session Outline

**10.1 The First Tremors: Subprime Cracks and the August 2007 Freeze.** The decline of the ABX index, the failure of two Bear Stearns hedge funds, the freeze in the ABCP market, and the first central bank interventions.

**10.2 Bear Stearns and the March 2008 Rescue.** The run on Bear Stearns, the Fed-arranged acquisition by JPMorgan Chase, and the introduction of emergency lending facilities for investment banks.

**10.3 The September 2008 Panic.** The conservatorship of Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac, the decision to let Lehman Brothers fail, the rescue of AIG, and the consequences of these decisions for market confidence.

**10.4 The Run on the Shadow Banking System.** The breaking of the buck by the Reserve Primary Fund, the freeze in commercial paper markets, the run on repo, and the functional equivalent of the banking panics of 1930-1933.

**10.5 Global Contagion.** How the crisis spread from the United States to Europe, Asia, and the emerging markets through financial linkages, trade channels, and confidence effects.

**10.6 Transmission to the Real Economy.** The credit crunch, the collapse of trade, the wealth effect, and the uncertainty channel through which the financial crisis produced the Great Recession.
