The cryptocurrency industry experienced a spectacular series of crises in 2022 that collectively destroyed approximately $2 trillion in market value and demonstrated that the financial fragilities analyzed throughout this course, bank runs, Ponzi dynamics, leverage cycles, fraud, and contagion, can reproduce themselves in new technological settings with remarkable fidelity. The collapse of the Terra/Luna stablecoin ecosystem in May 2022, the cascade of failures among crypto lending platforms in the summer of 2022, and the fraud-driven collapse of the FTX exchange in November 2022 were not genuinely novel phenomena. They were, in essential structure, repetitions of patterns that have recurred since the South Sea Bubble.

This session examines the cryptocurrency crises of 2022 through the analytical lens developed in earlier sessions. The objective is not to provide a comprehensive survey of cryptocurrency technology but to demonstrate that the theoretical frameworks of financial crises, Minsky dynamics, Diamond-Dybvig runs, information asymmetries, moral hazard, and contagion, apply with full force to the crypto ecosystem, and that the absence of the regulatory safeguards that were painstakingly developed after previous crises (deposit insurance, capital requirements, disclosure rules, auditing standards) allowed those fragilities to produce their natural consequences.

## Learning Objectives

After completing this session, you should be able to:

1. Identify the structural parallels between cryptocurrency market dynamics and the historical episodes of speculation and crisis studied in earlier sessions.
2. Explain the mechanics of the Terra/Luna collapse as a case of a fragile monetary peg, and compare it to the currency crises studied in Session 7.
3. Trace the contagion chain from Terra/Luna through Three Arrows Capital, Celsius, Voyager, and BlockFi, identifying the common creditor and leverage channels.
4. Analyze the FTX collapse as a case of fraud enabled by the absence of regulatory oversight, and compare it to the corporate governance failures of Session 8.
5. Evaluate the financial stability risks of decentralized finance (DeFi), including procyclical liquidation mechanisms and oracle dependencies.
6. Assess the policy options for addressing cryptocurrency risks, including regulation, stablecoin frameworks, and central bank digital currencies.

## Session Outline

**14.1 Cryptocurrency Markets and the Echoes of Financial History.** The crypto boom of 2020-2021, the Kindleberger-Minsky parallels, and the structural features of the crypto ecosystem that made it vulnerable to crisis.

**14.2 Stablecoin Fragility: Terra/Luna and the Mechanics of a Death Spiral.** The design of algorithmic stablecoins, the Terra/Luna collapse of May 2022, and the comparison to currency pegs and money market fund runs.

**14.3 Contagion in Crypto.** The cascade of failures from Terra/Luna through Three Arrows Capital, Celsius, Voyager, and BlockFi, and the channels of contagion within the crypto ecosystem.

**14.4 FTX: Fraud, Commingling, and the Absence of Regulation.** The rise and fall of FTX, the commingling of customer funds with Alameda Research, and the parallels to earlier financial frauds.

**14.5 DeFi: Automated Financial Fragility.** The financial stability risks of decentralized finance protocols, including procyclical liquidation cascades, composability risk, and oracle manipulation.

**14.6 Policy Responses: Regulation, CBDCs, and the Future of Digital Finance.** The regulatory debate over cryptocurrency, stablecoin regulation frameworks, and the potential role of central bank digital currencies.
